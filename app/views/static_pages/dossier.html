Paramilitary Operations Officer � Special Operations Group (SOG) � Afghanistan

Paramilitary Operations Officers are veterans of clandestine operations, often hailing from the Special Forces and intelligence elements of the United States Armed Forces. For the members of the Special Operations Group, their focus is on acquiring intelligence and conducting risky operations in a highly lethal environment, operating in small, deniable teams that the US government will not associate with if compromised. The Group in Afghanistan are veterans of the War on Terror and the brutal counterinsurgency being waged against the Taliban, with many having been on the front lines when the United States invaded and taking part in numerous battles from Tora Bora to Kandahar. This Group will know Afghanistan well and have numerous contacts among the US military there, local Afghan government personal and even with locals whose actions might be of questionable legality. This Group will be working closely with the SOG in Pakistan to better manage the US War on Terror as well as to more comprehensively engage Islamic militants on the ground. This Group is authorized to expand their network of contacts as necessary in order to fulfill their mission. This Group will be the primary agent in covert operations being conducted in Afghanistan and may assist elsewhere when necessary.

Counterterrorism Analysts are pivotal in understanding the structures, financing, tradecraft, recruitment, capabilities and efforts of terrorist groups and their sponsors, whether they are state or non-state. Their understanding of the various insurgencies and terrorist groups in Central Asia will be pivotal as the United States attempts to outmaneuver the large variety of actors all competing for influence in the region.

Leadership Analysts are more or less focused on the main players running each government or organization.
